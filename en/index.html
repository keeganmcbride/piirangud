Restrictions in force

ET RU

People continue to have an obligation to wear a mask or cover their nose and mouth in public indoor spaces, including public transportation.

The obligation to wear a mask or cover one's nose and mouth is in force in public indoor spaces, including public transportation and service facilities (except for children under the age of 12 or in cases where wearing a mask is not possible due to health considerations, special needs, the nature of the work or activity, or some other substantial reason). A public indoor space is a space designated for public use that can be entered by anyone wishing to do so, without, for instance, a requirement to register in advance; it is a space densely populated with people who do not come into daily contact with each other.

The 2+2 rule is still in force in all public indoor spaces

The so-called 2+2 rule must be followed in all public indoor spaces, which means that up to two people may move around together, keeping a distance of at least 2 metres from other people. For instance, this rule is in force in the bank office, museum, exhibitions, hair and beauty salons, the lobbies of accommodation providers but also in water parks, swimming pools, saunas. The restriction does not apply to a family moving around together, or if it is not possible to ensure this reasonably, for instance if there are many passengers in public transportation, where, in addition to keeping a maximum possible distance, a mask must also be worn.

A 50% maximum occupancy requirement still applies in the sales space of a store, service area of a service provider and the common spaces of a commercial establishment

It must be ensured that the occupancy does not exceed 50% and that the so-called 2+2 rule is followed within that 50% occupancy in all indoor stores, service areas of service providers and common spaces of commercial establishments. This means that first it must be calculated how much 50% of the floor space of the room in public use is and, as customers also have to follow the 2+2 rule, this 50% must be divided by 4 so that each customer would get 4m2.

From March 1 to March 28, distance learning will continue in general education schools, vocational schools and institutions of higher education. Regular schoolwork will only take place for students in grades 1 to 4.

Starting from March 15, the students who are taking the basic school final exams and state exams in the 2020/2021 academic year will be allowed to have contact learning two days a week. The schools have to implement measures necessary for preventing the spread of the virus in accordance with the guidelines of the Ministry of Education and Research.

It is only allowed to be in school buildings in special cases: if the students require educational support services or consultations to achieve learning results, are carrying out practical learning, taking exams or tests, or participating in Olympiads, The restrictions also do not apply to students with educational special needs or students who need support services.

Doing sports, training, youth work, hobby activities and refresher training are allowed indoors as a contact free individual activity or individual training, including with an instructor. Indoor group activities or training are not allowed.

Starting from March 3, the allowed indoor occupancy will go down to 25%.

From March 6, sports competitions and exercise events are allowed only for professional athletes and exercise events are also allowed for people with special needs.

From March 3, outdoor sports, training, youth work, hobby activities, informal education and refresher training are subject to the restriction of 10 people per group, instead of the 50 people per group restriction in force thus far. The group may not come into contact with other groups.

Spas, saunas, water parks and swimming facilities will remain closed until March 28, it is allowed to use swimming pools for individual swimming.

During the period of March 3 to March 28, it is not allowed to organise indoor public events, including cinema showings, concerts, theatre performances, or conferences, but also public worship services and other religious services. It is also not allowed to be in places where entertainment services are provided, e.g. casino, night club, bowling or pool hall, or children's playrooms, museums and exhibition venues.

Outdoor public events and public meetings are allowed in groups of up to 10 people and it must be ensured that different groups do not come into contact with each other. It is not allowed to organise public events between the hours of 21.00 and 06.00.

A 25% occupancy limit will go into effect for catering and commercial establishments. Commercial and catering establishments must be closed on weekends, on weekdays, the catering establishments must close their doors at 18.00. Selling food for takeaway is still allowed. Grocery stores, and stores for essential goods, e.g. pharmacies and opticians may stay open, as may stores that allow for so-called drive-in selling. A requirement to spread out the customers will go into force for open air selling spaces, e.g. markets.
